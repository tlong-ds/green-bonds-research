Answer: ❌ NO - Feature selection (02) is NOT necessary for causal inference

Why not:

  Feature Selection (02)        vs    Causal Inference (03)
  ─────────────────────────            ────────────────────
  Goal: Predict accurately            Goal: Estimate unbiased effects
  Method: Data-driven filtering       Method: Theory-driven specification
  Logic: Remove low-signal vars       Logic: Keep all confounders
  Best for: ML, forecasting           Best for: Causal inference
  
  ⚠️ Risk: Removes confounders       ✅ Benefit: Preserves identification
     → Omitted variable bias             → Unbiased causal estimates


Key Econometric Principle:
────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────

Econometric models are specified from KNOWLEDGE, not DATA.

  PSM specification: "Which variables determine treatment assignment?"
                     ← Answer from causal theory, NOT correlation analysis
  
  DiD specification: "What controls ensure parallel trends?"
                     ← Answer from economic mechanism, NOT auto-selection
